"An engenier at heart. His tools being his violin, his bow and his insatiable curiosity"

Guillermo was born in Mexico city in a family of music-lovers. He learned piano with Isolda Stepanova and violin with Alexander Klochkov. With only 16 years of age, he held an accademist position at the Carlos Chavez Symphony Orchestra, with whom he later debuted as soloist playing J. S. Bach´s A minor violin concerto. Guillermo was granted the “Kyra and Douglas Downie” and “Linda Hill” awards for his musical talent, after being accepted at the Royal College of Music in 2010 where he studied with Daniel Rowland. He later concluded a Masters degree and postgraduate studies at Mozarteum University with Vittorio Ghielmi and Reinhard Goebel. During his studies, Guillermo closely learnt from and collaborated with members of the Academy of Ancient Music, the Orchestra of the Age of Enlightenment and the Mahler Chamber Orchestra, like Steven Devine, Jonathan Manson and Matthew Truscott, as well as well as with members of "Il Giardino Armonico" and the english period ensemble "Florilegium".

Guillermo has been a worthy participant at the Hermilo Novelo violin competition 2007 and the Vivaldi-Vienna string competiton 2019. He had taken part in a variaty of music festivals, both as soloist and chamber musician, including the Cevantino Festival, London Haendel Festival, Innsbrucker Festwoche Der Alten Musik, Im Dialog Festival and Jazz and the City. Guillermo performs regularily with the austrian ensamble "Bach Werk Vokal", diving in the vast world of Bach's vocal music, and with "The Van Swietens", with whom he recently has taken part in an unprecedented recording of the 3rd and 4rd Beethoven/Lachner Piano Concertos with string chamber orchestra, and in collaboration with other young chamber musicians, including members of the Hanson and Adelphi Quartets. Guillermo is a dedicated pedagogue working both as violin teacher and in regular workshops throughout the year, like the "KlangKarton" concert series for children, organized by the Stiftung Mozarteum. He has also taken part in workshops with an emphasis on inclusivity for the austrian association "Spiel Raum Musik".
